USACE Regulatory Workflow

Project Tracker for PMs

USACE Wilmington District CESAW

Track each project by entering a start date, selecting a project type, and marking the review items as Yes or No. The dashboard auto-calculates days in review, target decision dates, and progress percentages. Status shows ⚠️ ⚠️ for incomplete rows, 👍 💯 for fully completed rows whether all yes or mixed, and 👎 🛑 when all responses are no.

Show typical regulatory timelines reference
PCN, Pre-Construction Notification 45 day Corps review window after receipt of a complete PCN.
NWP, Nationwide Permit Often immediate if no PCN is required, 45 days when a PCN is required.
UA, Unauthorized Action No single definitive timeline, depends on enforcement posture, restoration needs, and submittal completeness.
IP, Individual Permit Common tracking goal is about 120 days once the application is complete and the review process is underway.
SIP, Standard Individual Permit Similar to IP, commonly tracked at about 120 days for workload management.
Permit Mod, Permit Modification Often tracked around 45 days, but can vary with the scope of the requested change.
DC, Delineation Concurrence Common tracking window is same day to about 30 days, depending on submittal quality and review needs.
AJD, Approved Jurisdictional Determination Common Wilmington-style tracking goal is about 60 days.
PJD, Preliminary Jurisdictional Determination Often same day to about 30 days if the request package is sufficient.
PREAPPCONS, Pre-Application Consultation Common planning and coordination target is about 30 days.
NPR, Notice / Public Review Public notice comment windows are typically tracked at 30 days.
PGP, Programmatic General Permit Common tracker value is about 45 days, though actual timing depends on the partner program and completeness.
RGP, Regional General Permit Common tracker value is about 60 days, used as an expedited regional general permit review benchmark.
LOP, Letter of Permission Common abbreviated review track is about 60 to 90 days, this tracker uses 90 days.
